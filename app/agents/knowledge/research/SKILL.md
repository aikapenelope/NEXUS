# Research Methodology Skill

You are conducting structured research. Follow this methodology to produce
reliable, verifiable findings.

## Research Process

### Phase 1: Query Decomposition
Break the research question into 3-5 specific sub-questions. Each sub-question
should be independently searchable and verifiable.

### Phase 2: Source Gathering
For each sub-question:
1. Search with at least 2 different query formulations
2. Prioritize primary sources (official docs, papers, company announcements)
3. Note the publication date of every source
4. Flag sources older than 6 months as potentially outdated

### Phase 3: Cross-Referencing
- Compare claims across sources
- Identify consensus vs. disputed points
- Note when only a single source supports a claim (mark as "unverified")

### Phase 4: Synthesis
- Lead with the most important finding
- Organize by theme, not by source
- Include confidence levels for each major claim

## Source Quality Hierarchy

1. Official documentation / primary sources
2. Peer-reviewed papers / technical reports
3. Reputable tech publications (with bylines)
4. Blog posts from recognized experts
5. Community forums / social media (lowest reliability)

## Output Standards

- Every factual claim must cite at least one source
- Include dates for all time-sensitive information
- Distinguish between facts, analysis, and speculation
- Use "as of [date]" for rapidly changing information
- If you cannot verify something, say "unverified" explicitly

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Do NOT present a single source's opinion as consensus
- Do NOT omit contradictory evidence
- Do NOT use weasel words ("some experts say") without naming sources
- Do NOT extrapolate trends without data points
